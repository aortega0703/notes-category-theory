\subsubsection{Product Functor}

\begin{definition}[Product Functor]\index{Product Functor}

  For a category $\C$ with all binary products, it is possible to define a
  functor $\times:\C\times \C \to \C$ composed by maps
  of~\parencite[p.~47]{awodey:category_theory}:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Objects:
      \[\big(\forall (C,D)\in (\C\times \C)_0\big)
        \big(\times(C, D) = C\times D\big)\]
    \item Morphisms:
      \[\big(\forall (f, g)\in (\C\times \C)_1\big)
        \big(\times(f, g) = f\times g\big)\]
  \end{itemize}
\end{definition}

  For a category $\C$, the right adjoint of the product functor $\hole \times
  A: \C\to \C$ is the exponential functor $[A, \hole]:\C\to \C$.
